Week Four

Week Four Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this week, students will be able to:
  • Describe the evolution of the process quality concept, and the difference between process and process quality.
  • Understand how to align a process with a selected certification model.
  • Be able to describe the present state of certification paradigms and the fallacy of certification.

Week Four Assignments

Homework consists of reading the text chapters and writing an evaluation on each of the chapters assigned for that week. Key to successfully completing the homework is to identify the main concepts of the chapter and to relate those concepts in contemporary terms. Grading is based on the completeness of your work and a demonstration of your understanding of the main elements of the chapter. Be succinct in your writing; you will need to cover the chapter's main ideas in one single-spaced page (TNR, 12 Point Font, 1" boarders). Homework is due weekly on Sunday. The forth week is due Friday. Make the filename your last name and a number for the week (i.e.: LASTNAME2.docx for the second week assignments). Late or incomplete homework may receive partial credit at the discretion of the professor.

Homework Chapters 8 and 9

Read the text chapters and write an evaluation on each. Identify the main concepts of the chapter and relate to those concepts in contemporary terms. Be succinct in your writing and cover the chapter's main ideas in one single-spaced page per chapter (TNR, 12 Point Font, 1" boarders).

Group Project

Each group will develop a Quality Assurance Plan from a template provided by the professor. The Quality Assurance Plan will be based on a requirements document provided by the professor or provided by the team. Details will be provided in class.

Individual Presentation

Submit your individual presentation (PowerPoint format with 4 – 6 slides) at least two days prior to the day you are presenting. Use your last name as the file name (i.e.: Appel.ppt) Each student will be responsible for making a five-minute presentation for his or her selected paper. You will give an oral presentation on the research followed by a Q&A. A successful individual presentation will present a synthesis of the major concepts, discussion of those concepts in today's terms, and a personal evaluation. Schedule your presentation date with the instructor on the first night of class. Please see the presentation section for criteria for all presentation evaluations.

Click on the "Week Four Assignment" link above to submit your assignment, as well as to get more information regarding the due date and grading rubric.

Weekly Status Report
Weekly Status Report

All teams will submit a team status report weekly to the professor in an email. Teams will address the accomplishments from last week, goals for next week, and issues with several options for addressing those issues (if any). Be brief in your team status report email. During week four and after sending the fourth status report, copy the contents of all four weekly status reports into one MS word document and submit by clicking on the link above.
